Introduction & Objective

The Mission

The Kepler Labelled Time Series Dataset originates from the Kepler Space Telescope mission, which continuously monitored thousands of distant stars. It recorded precise changes in their brightness (flux) over long durations to detect transiting exoplanets.

Project Objective

The main objective is to demonstrate how data-science techniques can be effectively applied to real astronomical measurements using Python-based analytical tools (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib). We aim to detect recurring dips in brightness that indicate a planet passing in front of its host star.

Conclusion & Future Scope

Conclusion

The Kepler mission's long-term dataset has played a central role in transforming how astronomers detect and classify exoplanets. By loading, exploring, and visualizing Kepler-derived CSV data, this work demonstrates the fundamental workflow behind identifying potential planetary signals.

Future Scope

Future extensions could involve incorporating neural-network architectures, implementing real-time detection pipelines, or integrating more refined noise-correction methods. Expanding this prototype into an automated framework would enhance the speed of identifying planetary signatures.
